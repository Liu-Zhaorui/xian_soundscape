# Mingde Gate Soundscape

## Historical place

Mingde Gate was the outer southern gate of Sui–Tang Chang’an, with five parallel passages anchoring the capital’s north–south axis. Beyond lay open ground; inside lay the ward grid—sound shifted from field to gate to neighborhood.

## Layers of sound

- **Passages**: Parallel tunnels create slightly different reverberance and imaging as you move.
- **Carts and markets**: Midday traffic and calls stack in the midrange; walking near a wall adds reflection.
- **Rite and routine**: Ceremonies and everyday passage could alternate in the same space with very different levels and rhythm.

## Visiting tip

Try mapping dominant sound directions: wind from the south, lateral reflections from east–west streets, and how modern traffic seems gathered or filtered when you stand in the gate zone.
